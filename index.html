Types of Prosthetic Organs

Upper Limb Prosthetics

Upper limb prosthetics are designed to replace arms, hands, and fingers lost due to amputation or congenital limb deficiency. They come in various designs, ranging from simple cosmetic devices to advanced myoelectric prostheses that enable precise control and natural movements.

Lower Limb Prosthetics

Lower limb prosthetics are used to replace legs, feet, and toes lost due to amputation or other conditions. They help individuals regain mobility and stability, allowing them to walk, run, and engage in various activities with confidence and ease.

Specialized Prosthetic Organs

In addition to upper and lower limb prosthetics, there are specialized prosthetic organs designed for specific purposes. These include prosthetic eyes, ears, noses, and other facial prostheses used for cosmetic enhancement or functional restoration.

Types of Procedures

  • Prosthetic Fitting

    Prosthetic fitting is the process of customizing and attaching a prosthetic device to the residual limb of an individual. This involves taking precise measurements, selecting appropriate components, and ensuring a comfortable and secure fit. Additional details about the prosthetic fitting process can go here.

  • Prosthetic Maintenance

    Prosthetic maintenance involves regular care and upkeep of the prosthetic device to ensure optimal performance and longevity. This includes cleaning, lubricating, and inspecting the components for wear and tear. Additional details about prosthetic maintenance procedures can go here.

  • Prosthetic Rehabilitation

    Prosthetic rehabilitation focuses on helping individuals adapt to and maximize the use of their prosthetic devices. This may involve physical therapy, occupational therapy, and psychological support to enhance mobility, function, and overall well-being.
